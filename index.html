New York Prepares for Climate Change

By Sarah Kerson

Hunts Point Resiliency

The goal of the Hunts Point Energy Resiliency Project is to provide reliable and sustainable power to Hunts Point through energy generation and storage solutions. The city plans to implement a tri-generation microgrid to provide backup power to food buisnesses, solar power at two local schools, and mobile diesel generators.

Resilient East Harlem

The Resilient East Harlem plan aims to protect East Harlem, bordered by the Harlem River, from four risks of climate change: sea level rise, storm surge, extreme rain and extreme heat. The plan intends to acheive this by managing storm water, creating resilient public spaces, and adapting the waterfront.

East Side Coastal Resiliency Project

Funded by the city and the federal government, the East Side Coastal Resiliency Project aims to reduce flood risk from sea level rise and coastal storms on the east side of Manhattan from E. 25th Street to Montgomery Street. The project, which intends to raze East River Park and increase its height by eight feet, has become controversial with neighborhood residents. Neighborhood activists recently lost a legal battle to delay the project.

Lower Manhattan Coastal Resiliency

LMCR aims to protect Lower Manhattan from rising sea levels and coastal storms. The plan envisions a multilevel waterfront extending the East River shoreline. The multileveled park is designed to provide maximum protection from sea-level rise while providing New Yorkers with access to the waterfront.





Hurricane Irene

Hurricane Irene struck New York in August, 2011 and brought five to ten inches of rainfall. Central Park saw 72.81 inches of rainfall that year.

Superstorm Sandy

Superstorm Sandy devastated the tristate area in October of 2012. Fourty-four people died, and the storm caused $19 billion in damages. New York City saw 38.51 inches of rain that year.

Hurricanes Henri and Ida

The remnants of Hurricane Ida struck New York less than two weeks after Henri in August 2021, flooding Central Park with 3.1 inches of rain in just one hour. Ida became the deadliest storm to hit the New York area since Sandy. Thirteen people in the city died during the storm, many in unregulated basement apartments. Central Park saw 59.73 inches of rain in 2021.

New York City is uniquely positioned to feel the impacts of the climate crisis. From Superstorm Sandy, a wakeup call in 2012 that killed 44 people and caused $19 billion in damages, to the remnants of Hurricane Ida in 2021, which killed 13 people and exposed the dangers of unregulated basement apartments, it’s clear that the effects of climate change will only worsen.

Last year, the New York City Council passed a law that will require the mayor’s office and city government to develop a plan to mitigate “climate hazards,” including extreme weather and heat. The measure requires the plan to be updated every 10 years. The city is also working under OneNYC2050, a strategic plan to address the climate crisis.

The city already has several climate change resiliency projects underway.

In the Bronx, the Hunts Point Resiliency project aims to provide sustainable power to Hunts Point through energy generation and storage solutions. The project will implement a microgrid, solar power and storage at two public schools in the area, and mobile diesel generators.

Three ongoing projects in Manhattan will address the risks of the climate crisis on the island. Resilient East Harlem aims to reduce the neighborhood’s vulnerabilities to storm surge, sea level rise and extreme rain by managing stormwater, creating resilient public spaces and adapting the waterfront.

The embattled East Side Coastal Resiliency Project has faced strong opposition from neighborhood residents, who have organized against the city’s plan to tear down East River Park and rebuild it at a raised level. But community activists recently lost a court battle to halt the project, which aims to mitigate the effects of flooding and sea level rise from coastal storms.

The Lower Manhattan Coastal Resiliency project also aims to address the impacts of rising sea levels and coastal storms. The plan will extend the shoreline of the East River to a multilevel waterfront, with the goal of protecting the city from rising sea levels while providing residents with more access to the waterfront.
