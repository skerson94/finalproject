Hunts Point Resiliency

The goal of the Hunts Point Energy Resiliency Project is to provide reliable and sustainable power to Hunts Point through energy generation and storage solutions. The city plans to implement a tri-generation microgrid to provide backup power to food buisnesses, solar power at two local schools, and mobile diesel generators.

East Side Coastal Resiliency Project

Funded by the city and the federal government, the East Side Coastal Resiliency Project aims to reduce flood risk from sea level rise and coastal storms on the east side of Manhattan from E. 25th Street to Montgomery Street. The project, which intends to raze East River Park and increase its height by eight feet, has become controversial with neighborhood residents. Neighborhood activists recently lost a legal battle to delay the project.

Lower Manhattan Coastal Resiliency

LMCR aims to protect Lower Manhattan from rising sea levels and coastal storms. The plan envisions a multilevel waterfront extending the East River shoreline. The multileveled park is designed to provide maximum protection from sea-level rise while providing New Yorkers with access to the waterfront.





Hurricane Irene

Hurricane Irene struck New York in August, 2011 and brought five to ten inches of rainfall. Central Park saw 72.81 inches of rainfall that year.

Superstorm Sandy

Superstorm Sandy devastated the tristate area in October of 2012. Fourty-three people died, and the storm caused $19 billion in damages. New York City saw 38.51 inches of rain that year.

Hurricanes Henri and Ida

The remnants of Hurricane Ida struck New York less than two weeks after Henri in August 2021, flooding Central Park with 3.1 inches of rain in just one hour. Ida became the deadliest storm to hit the New York area since Sandy. Thirteen people in the city died during the storm, many in unregulated basement apartments. Central Park saw 59.73 inches of rain in 2021.
